The Six-Layer Problem: When Ownership Chains Are Designed to Exhaust the Investigator

Sophisticated beneficial ownership obfuscation doesn’t rely on any single technique. It relies on depth.

March 2026 8-minute read Locke & Boole Analyst Team

Sophisticated beneficial ownership obfuscation doesn’t rely on any single technique. It relies on depth — enough layers to make tracing feel more expensive than the alternative. Here’s how we approach it systematically.

What depth hides

Layered ownership makes it harder to see the ultimate beneficiary. Each additional entity adds paperwork, jurisdictional friction, and a new set of nominal officers or agents.

How we cut through layers

We use jurisdictional triangulation and cross-filing patterns to identify repeated names and common service providers. That lets us collapse the chain into the likely real ownership path.

The practice risk

Depth is effective only if the investigator accepts the effort. We treat each additional layer as a clue rather than a distraction, which turns the six-layer problem into a solvable structure.
