Boolean Search for Corporate Investigators: A Practical Reference

Advanced search operators are not a trick — they are the difference between noise and signal in open-source corporate investigation.

January 2026 6-minute read Locke & Boole Analyst Team

Advanced search operators are not a trick — they are the difference between a search that surfaces noise and one that surfaces signal. A reference guide to the operators we use most in daily investigation practice.

Why boolean matters

A well-crafted search string reduces false positives and surfaces the exact corporate names, service providers, or filings you need. It turns broad queries into targeted evidence-finding exercises.

Core operators

We use AND to intersect terms, OR to broaden a search, quotation marks for exact phrases, and minus signs to remove irrelevant results. Those operators are the foundation of every corporate intelligence search.

Investigator examples

A typical search might combine entity names, director names, and filing terms to identify related entities. Small tweaks to the query often produce dramatically better results.
